Travis Scott

Travis Scott, born Jacques Webster II on April 30, 1991, in Houston, Texas, grew up in a nearby suburb called Missouri City. He started making music as a teenager, first in a duo called the Graduates, then in another group called the Classmates. After dropping out of college, he moved to Los Angeles to focus on music. There, he met famous artist like T.I. and Kanye West, which helped him start working as a producer and appear on a popular music album in 2012.

Rise to Prominence

Travis Scott released his first project, Owl Pharaoh, in 2013, followed by Days Before Rodeo. His first album, Rodeo (2015), featured the hit “Antidote” and reached number three on the charts. In 2016, his second album, Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight, hit number one. He worked with big names like Kid Cudi, Kendrick Lamar, and Drake, and had another hit with “Butterfly effect” in 2017.

Astro world Festival Tragedy

On November 5th, 2021, during Travis Scott’s Astro world Festival in Houston, a crowd accident happened that sadly caused ten people to die and many others to get hurt. This caused a lot of criticism and legal problems. Despite this, Travis Scott came back to perform live shows in 2022, including one at the Billboard Music Awards.
